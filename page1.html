Amazon Rainforest Wildfire

The Amazon is one of the Earth's greatest contributors to the oxygen and carbon cycle. It produces about 60% of the world's oxygen and absorbs most of its carbon dioxide.

"
These fires are now not just an environmental catastrophe, they’re also potentially genocidal. By encouraging the land invaders and ranchers who set these fires, President Bolsonaro is signing a death warrant for the uncontacted tribes whose homes are going up in flames. If their forest is destroyed, they simply won’t survive.
"

-Stephen Corry (Survival International Director)

LOSS IN BIODIVERSITY

The millions of animals that live by the roots are also affected by the damages in the forest and won't be able to survive extreme heat from the fires. Aside from that tens of thousands of tree species and plants and hundreds of thousands of other wildlife were also devastated by the fire.

TRIBES IN THE AMAZON

Tribes like the Awá (Earth's Most Threatened Tribe), the Wau Wau, and many others are also pressured by illegal loggers, fires, and other threats which destroy their territories. The lives of more than a million indigenous people are at risk.

CLIMATE CHANGE

The fires contribute to carbon emissions. Rainforests play a vital role in the world’s ecosystem since they absorb heat instead of it being reflected back into the atmosphere. Amazon absorbs the carbon that humans continuously produce.

causes

Many have been blaming Brazil's president, President Bolsonaro, who decreased the IBAMA's (Brazil's environmental agency) by about 24% in April 2019. Hence, they were not able to monitor the forest and protect it from illegal loggers and those to start fires.

The fires are also being blamed on the cattle ranchers and loggers who use the Amazon's land for large meat production companies. They use the fires to clear areas of the Amazon to raise their cattle for money which is mostly likely to be the cause of the fire. Majority of the Amazon fires were human-lit, said Christian Poirier (program director of non-profit organization Amazon Watch).

The fires are releasing a great amount of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere while also destroying millions of trees that would have been able to convert all the carbon dioxide into oxygen and reverse the effects of the fires in the atmosphere.
