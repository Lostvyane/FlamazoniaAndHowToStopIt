The Amazon rainforest is the biggest, most important, rainforest in the word. It is the home to about a fourth of the world's land animals. If the forest continues to burn, it will turn into an entirely different ecosystem unsuitable for its the animals and plants to survive.

The Earth depends on the Amazon as one of its last sanctuaries for many species like jaguars, harpy eagles, and pink river dolphins, and it is home to sloths, black spider monkeys, and poison dart frogs much more. One out of 10 species consider the Amazon rainforest as their home. These add up to about 40,000 plant species, 3,000 fresh fish species, and more than 370 types of reptiles. For a span of 20, over 2,000 new species in the Amazon have been described.

endangered animals

of the amazon rainforest

Hyacinth Macaw
The Amazonian rain forest is a home to various different species which includes the blue-and-yellow macaw. Macaws are known to be highly intelligent, and they can live for up to 60 years. The color and pattern of their feathers are what makes them distinguishable and popular among the species in the illegal pet trade. This pet trade has devastated and affected many populations of wild exotic birds.
Giant Otters
The giant otters are dependent on both land and water for survival. They can grow up to 1.7 meters long. They became endangered due to deforestation and water contamination.
Uakari Monkey
Uakari monkeys are known for their bright red face. The name Uakari originates from the Uakari tribe that once lived in the Amazon but is now extinct. Due to extensive deforestation, it put the monkey on the endangered list.
